The 12753 / 54 Hazur Sahib Nanded Hazrat Nizamuddin Marathwada Sampark Kranti Express is a Superfast express train of the Sampark Kranti Express series belonging to Indian Railways - South Central Railway zone that runs between Hazur Sahib Nanded and Hazrat Nizamuddin in India.

It operates as train number 12753 from Hazur Sahib Nanded to Hazrat Nizamuddin and as train number 12754 in the reverse direction serving the states of Maharashtra, Madhya Pradesh, Uttar Pradesh and Delhi.

It is the latest train introduced in the Sampark Kranti Express series which were originally started by the then railway minister of India Mr. Nitish Kumar [1].

Coaches

The 12753 / 54 Hazur Sahib Nanded Hazrat Nizamuddin Marathwada Sampark Kranti Express has one AC 2 tier, two AC 3 tier, six Sleeper Class, four Unreserved/General and two Seating cum Luggage Rake Coaches. It does not carry a Pantry car coach.[2]

As is customary with most train services in India, coach composition may be amended at the discretion of Indian Railways depending on demand.
Service

The 12753 Hazur Sahib Nanded Hazrat Nizamuddin Marathwada Sampark Kranti Express covers the distance of 1,633 km (1,015 mi) in 29 hours 00 mins averaging 56.31 km/h (35 mph)[3] and in 29 hours 10 mins as 12754 Hazrat Nizamuddin Hazur Sahib Nanded Marathwada Sampark Kranti Express averaging 55.99 km/h (35 mph) [3].

As the average speed of the train is above 55 km/h (34 mph), as per Indian Railway rules, its fare includes a Superfast surcharge.
Routeing

The Express runs from Hazur Sahib Nanded via Parbhani Junction, Jalna, Aurangabad, Manmad Junction, Jalgaon Junction, Bhopal Junction, Jhansi Junction, Agra Cantonment to Hazrat Nizamuddin.[2][4]

Additionally, it has a technical halt at Bhusaval Junction.
Traction

As the entire route yet to be fully electrified, a Pune based WDP 4[5] hauls the train from Hazur Sahib Nanded up to Manmad Junction handing over to a Bhusaval based WAP 4 which powers the train for the remainder of its journey.
Operation

The Marathwada Sampark Kranti Express runs from Hazur Sahib Nanded every Tuesday reaching Hazrat Nizamuddin the next day .i.e. Wednesday.[3] It runs from Hazrat Nizamuddin every Wednesday reaching Hazur Sahib Nanded on the 2nd day .i.e. Friday.[6]
